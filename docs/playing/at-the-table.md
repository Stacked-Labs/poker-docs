# At the table

Once the Host approves you, you're seated and the next hand deals you in.

## Acting on a hand

When the action is on you, you have the standard set of moves — fold, check, call, bet, or raise. You see your hand, the community cards as they come, and the current bet to match.

Each decision has an **action timer** — a countdown that starts when it's your turn. If you don't act before it expires, the system folds you. The timer applies to every decision, including the showdown.

Your actions during a hand are off-chain: real-money or Free Play, while a hand is in progress nothing is being signed by your wallet. The blockchain only sees the result of the hand once it settles.

## Sit out and come back

If you need a break — bathroom, phone call, doorbell — click **sit out**. You stay seated and your stack stays at the table, but you're dealt out of upcoming hands. When you're ready, click **I'm back** to rejoin the next deal.

Sitting out is the right move for short breaks. For longer ones, leave the table cleanly and rejoin later.

## Chat

Every table has a text chat among seated players. Use it for table talk or whatever culture the table runs on. Free Play and real-money tables both have chat.

## Between hands

Between hands, real-money tables briefly show an on-chain settlement indicator while the contract receives the previous hand's outcome. It's normally instant — by the time the next deal starts, settlement is done. The indicator links through to the transaction on Basescan if you want to verify a specific hand.

Free Play tables don't have this — nothing settles on-chain.

## Leaving the table

When you're done, click **leave** between hands (you can't leave mid-hand — finish the current one first). The contract grants you withdrawal permission and you click withdraw to pull your stack back to your wallet. Full walkthrough on [Withdrawals](/docs/your-money/withdrawals).
